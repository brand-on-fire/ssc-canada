Service Introduction: General Guardianship Services for the whole year, including the first day of landing in Vancouver, the first day of the Education Bureau/school placement, logistical support with important documents, attendance of each semester parent-teacher meeting, and ongoing communication with the school.  The role of the contact person is to be informed by the school and provide the relevant information to the parents.

Applicable to the crowd: Students have a good level of English; strong ability to live and learn to be independent; have a strong opinion; often communicate with their parents, often listening to parental advice; strong ability to adapt to the new environment; outgoing students. (Otherwise, it is recommended to choose full-scale guardian service throughout the year)

Guardian Custodianship

If you are considered a minor in Canada, defined as under the age of 18 (19 in British Columbia and New Brunswick), you will require a custodian in order to obtain your study permit from Citizenship and Immigration Canada (CIC).

If you are seen as a minor in Canada and you are not accompanied by a legal Guardian studying in Canada, then you will need to have a guardian, Custodian, to issue a student visa. This guardian must be Canadian nationals or residents with permanent residence. Even if you do not need a study visa (time is less than 6 months), according to SSC-Canada’s requirements, all minors, as long as they are not accompanied by their parents and are studying or traveling in Canada alone, they must accept the service of SSC-Canada. Both students with and without a study permit visa need a local guardian (Custodian).

For related information please see Citizenship & Immigration Canada’s “Studying in Canada – Minors”

Please note that students care who custodians is different from the method given guardianship of people parents guardians.

Required Statutory Notarization Letters for Studying Visas:

  • The first notarized document must be signed by the parents or legal guardians of the minor child in the child’s country of origin. Legal Notarization Letter Authorized by a Parent or Legal Guardian Visa
  • The second notarized document must be signed by the custodian in Canada. Legal Notarization Letter Signed by the Acting Guardian in Canada

Canada ‘s definition of minors

Province/Territory Age of Majority
Alberta, Manitoba, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, Saskatchewan 18
British Columbia, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Northwest Territories, Nova Scotia, Nunavut, Yukon 19

Guardian application Process:

  1. Parents or legal guardians must sign a “Parental Consent to Custodianship”, granting SSC-Canada the authority to act as the student’s custodian. This form will be sent to the student after they have submitted their application. This document must be notarized in your home country and sent to SSC-Canada.
  2.  Upon receipt of your notarized Parental Consent and after payment of the necessary fees, SSC-Canada will send you a notarized document, signed by the custodian in Canada, declaring that SSC-Canada has accepted the role of custodian for you.
  3.  In addition to your Letter of Acceptance from your school, you will take these documents when you apply for your study permit.  The purpose of this entire process is to satisfy the CIC that adequate arrangements have been made for the care and support for minor children who travel to Canada to study.

Study monitoring human duty and responsibility of any

The Canadian law governing defines the obligations of a custodian to:

  • Make adequate arrangements for the care and support of the student and
  • Reside within a reasonable distance of the minor child’s intended residence and school.

Pupils

All primary school students attending the Vancouver School District must live with their parents or adult immediate family members (such as grandparents, aunts, aunts, aunts, aunts, uncles, uncles). Student Permit Students must obtain a valid student visa before leaving their place of residence to attend various schools under the Vancouver Board of Education. Students must apply for a student visa on their own from the Canadian embassy or consulate in their place of origin. For more information, please contact the Canadian Embassy or inquiry

Health and accident insurance

British Columbia requires comprehensive health and accident insurance. Every overseas student must have this guarantee while studying in Vancouver. Overseas students can receive medical and hospital benefits through the government’s Medical Service Plan (MSP).
